Privacy Policy

Last updated: 28 June 2026

VORSO is a fitness platform built around card-based games, with a social layer where you can follow other people, share posts, compete on leaderboards, and cheer each other on. We believe your data belongs to you. This policy explains exactly what we collect, why, where it lives, who can see it, and what rights you have over it.

1. Who We Are

VORSO ("we", "us", "our") operates the VORSO mobile application and the website at vorso.org. For any privacy enquiries, contact us at customerfeedback@vorso.org.

3. What Stays on Your Device vs. What We Store

On your device (and your private iCloud): your workout history, streaks, XP, personal bests, and profile photo. This is synced only between your device and your own iCloud account — it is not sent to VORSO's servers and is not accessible to us.

On our servers (AWS, US-East): your account, username, age, optional profile details, and — when you use the social and leaderboard features — your scores, posts, comments, likes, follows, and reports. This is what makes leaderboards and the feed work across users.

4. Social Features & What Others Can See

VORSO is social by design. When you use these features, other people can see certain information:

  • Your username, bio, and country appear on your public profile.
  • Your leaderboard scores and times appear on leaderboards — only if you keep "Appear on leaderboards" turned on. Turn it off any time in Settings to remove yourself.
  • Posts, comments, likes, and shared sessions are visible to people who can see your activity. You can delete any of your own content at any time.
  • Setting your account to Private means people must send a follow request before they can follow you and see your activity.
  • You can block anyone (they can no longer follow you, see you in friend leaderboards, or interact with you) and report users or content.

Your real name and email are never shown to other users — only your chosen username.

5. Content Moderation & Safety

VORSO has zero tolerance for objectionable content or abusive behaviour. We provide in-app tools to block users and report users, posts, and comments. We review reports and take action — including removing content and disabling accounts — and we aim to act on reports of objectionable content within 24 hours. Continued or serious violations result in permanent removal from the Service. See our Terms of Service for the full content policy.

6. Third-Party Services We Use

We use a small number of carefully selected services:

  • Amazon Web Services (AWS) — AWS Cognito handles sign-in authentication (stores your email); AWS DynamoDB and related services store your account, leaderboard, and social data in the US-East region. Amazon's privacy policy: aws.amazon.com/privacy
  • Resend — delivers one-time sign-in codes and feedback confirmation emails. Email content passes through Resend transiently and is not retained by them. resend.com/legal/privacy-policy
  • Cloudflare — proxies our website for security and performance and may process request metadata (IP address, headers). cloudflare.com/privacypolicy

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third party for marketing or commercial purposes.

7. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), UK, or Switzerland, we process your personal data under the following legal bases:

  • Contract performance — your email, username, and age are necessary to provide the account, leaderboard, and social services you signed up for.
  • Legitimate interests — keeping the community safe (moderation), preventing abuse, and responding to feedback.
  • Consent — optional profile details, marketing communications, and waitlist sign-ups; you can withdraw at any time.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction — edit your username, bio, and profile details in-app, or ask us to correct data.
  • Deletion ("right to be forgotten") — delete your account directly in the app (Profile → Delete my account), which erases your account, username, profile, scores, posts, comments, follows, and other social data from our servers. You can also email us.
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection / Restriction — object to or restrict certain processing activities.
  • California (CCPA) — California residents have the right to know, delete, and opt-out of the sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information.

To exercise any of these rights, email customerfeedback@vorso.org. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Data Security

Authentication is managed through AWS Cognito, which enforces industry-standard security including encrypted storage and TLS in transit. Our backend runs on AWS with access controls and encrypted storage. Our website is served over HTTPS with HSTS enforced. We do not store passwords — sign-in uses one-time codes only.

10. Age Requirement

VORSO is intended for adults. You must be 18 or older to create an account or use the Service, as set out in our Terms of Service. We collect your date of birth solely to verify this. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 18. If you believe someone under 18 has provided us with their data, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

11. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you by email. Continued use of VORSO after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
